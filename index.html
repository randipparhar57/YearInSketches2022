The inspiration behind this project

Hello reader! I hope you are fine and dandy! Welcome to 2022 - A Year in Sketches. The idea for this project came to me in November of 2022 on Thanksgiving Day. My sister was working, and my parents had a dinner planned with some of their friends. To entertain myself, I started rummaging through my stuff and came across the postcard you see below (without the writing, that came afterwards.) I had bought this postcard in a shop on Bainbridge Island in Washington State back in February of 2022 with the intention of giving it to a friend of mine. I had forgotten to, and in all honesty, had forgotten about the postcard all together, until I came across it that night.

I started thinking about how the year was coming to an end in a matter of weeks. It was a tumultuous journey. 2022 will be remembered as one of the more defining years of my life. I experienced many new things and gained great memories. At the same time, those memories were being overcast by the rougher periods of my year. I wanted to find a way to pay homage to all of it though, because every single moment and memory has played an important part in shaping who I am as a person and the trajectory of my life.

And so, I picked up the postcard and a pencil and started drawing to capture the key moments of the last year. I opened YouTube and Spotify and started listening to the music that was most played during those time periods to travel back to specific moments and memories that are near and dear to me. And little by little, as I opened my mind and heart, I was reliving the defining moments of the past year.

This project would have been more complete if I had the journal that I kept during this time. Unfortunately, I lost it. That journal, in addition to my entries, also had mementos like concert tickets, receipts from my trip to Seattle, receipts from times that I hung out with other people, little cards, stuff like that. Now that I don't have those things, it almost seems as none of those things ever happened.

Initially, I was going to recreate those journal entries. However, the rewritten entries were not as authentic, raw, and honest as the originals. Hindsight takes over instead. And frankly, I’m not the best writer. Towards the end of the summer of 2022, I found the book “Daytripper” by Fàbio Moon and Gabriel Bà on a staircase of a brownstone in Brooklyn Heights. It was like kismet because the book ended up helping me through a period of transitions and changes. As I read the book, I came across so many excerpts that reminded me of moments that occurred throughout the year. And so, instead of journal entries, each memory will be accompanied by excerpts from “Daytripper” that remind me of those moments, whatever notes I have taken in my planner and phone during those times, and the music that was played during those time periods. Through creating these drawings, selecting excerpts from "Daytripper" and curating playlists of music I listened to most during those times, I was able to recreate those mementos - though they are not the original thing, the outcome is special in its own way. Something to keep in mind as you read the excerpts from “Daytripper” is not every entry is from my perspective, but of other people in my life. The nice thing about “Daytripper” is that it is a book that most everyone can relate to in their own unique way. I highly recommend reading it - the illustrations are evocative, and the writing is moving. It’s timeless in that it is relevant no matter what wave you are currently riding.

When I started this project, I never had any intention of sharing it with anyone. I started working on it to cope with some strong feelings I was experiencing at the time. The reason that I decided to share it upon its completion is because I recognize that I’m not the only one who feels like life is one, big sticky mess. Something that I’ve come to realize is that those sticky situations play an important part in our development and defining the trajectory of our life. In hindsight, those sticky situations are what differentiates between just existing versus living. I was hoping to have had this done by the end of December 2022 or beginning of January 2023, but I was busy with other aspects of life. I kind of feel silly for releasing this into the third month of 2023. However, finishing this project was necessary for me on a personal level. And, better late than never, right? I hope that my journey and this project helps bring joy or inspiration to those reading it.

Thank you to the people in my life who were there for me and saw the best in me, and for opening my eyes to a world that was under my nose this whole time. I wish you are all well, wherever you are.

With love,

Randip Parhar
